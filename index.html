    How Can I Stop a Repossession Order?

    Despite what you may have heard or read a repossession order can be stopped at any time before the eviction date.

    The options to avoid repossession of your house:
    1. Create a plan to settle your outstanding mortgage debt.
    2. Write to your lender to re-negotiate your mortgage.
    3. Apprise yourself of the free mortgage-rescue services you might qualify for.
    4. If a claim has already been filed a claim against you, then:
      • Prepare your evidence
      • Get legal representation for your repossession hearing.
    Is a cheaper mortgage available to you?

    You may need to re-finance to settle what’s owed to your current lender, but by transferring your mortgage elsewhere you may have lower monthly payments.

    If your lender gave you a temporary break, could you keep your repayment schedule and catch up?

    Then ask them to reduce or temporarily stop your re-payments for a short time. Alternatively it may be worth asking them to extend the term of your mortgage to reduce you payments ( though this will result in more interest being paid overall).

    Are you in a shared-ownership scheme?

    Look at selling back a portion of your ownership to the landlord to vary the terms of your flexible tenure.

    • Get the address and a name of the person or department handling your loan.
    • Avoid using emotional language, be polite and formal.
    • Summarise why you fell behind and how you can make up the payments.
    • Offer to pay a little extra for a few months if you can afford it.

  • Secured Finance is when the borrower has given their consent for a lender to place a legal charge over a certain asset or assets, such as a property, a vehicle or other tangible items which hold value. If the client serially defaults in payment, the lender has the legal right to seize control of the asset and sell it to settle the balance of the loan outstanding. There is a lower risk for a lender to offer secured finance and this should be reflected in the pay rate. Lenders quite often ask for some form of security if profit margins are tight, or if they want slightly more comfort with the arrangement to lend. With further security and increased lender comfort, lower interest rates are acquired for our clients.
